Dracaena angolensis

CLASSIFICATION

ORDER : Asparagales
FAMILY : Asparagaceae
GENUS : Dracaena
SPECIES : angolensis
BOTANICAL NAME : Dracaena angolensis (Welw.ex Carriere) Byng & Christenh.
SYNONYMS : ---
COMMON NAME : Spear Sansevieria.
TAMIL NAME : Marul
HINDI NAME : ---

PLANT DESCRIPTION

HABIT:  Rhizomatous succulent with rod-like leaves that alternate to form a fan-like shape, growing up to 0.9 - 1.2 m tall and 0.6 - 0.9 m wide.
LEAVES:  Stiff, cylindrical leaves taper to a sharp point and have a ribbed surface (0.6 - 1.5 m long, 2 - 3 cm wide). Leaves are greyish green with indistinct dark green bands that create a striped pattern.
FLOWERS:  White, fragrant flowers are tubular with 5 linear lobes curled backwards (2.5 cm long). The long, white stamens extend well beyond the lobes. Flowers are arranged in a whorled pattern along a spike-like inflorescence that is actually a raceme.

MEDICINAL USES

Numerous studies have investigated that this resinous medicine has antidiarrheal, antiulcer, antimicrobial, antiviral, antitumor, anti-inflammatory, analgesic, wound healing, and antioxidant activity.
